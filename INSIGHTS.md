Key Findings

1. Content Mix

Netflix’s catalog is predominantly composed of Movies, with TV Shows forming a smaller share.

This indicates a stronger historical emphasis on movie-based content over long-form episodic series.

2. Platform Growth Trend

Content additions increased significantly after 2015, coinciding with rapid global adoption of OTT platforms.

The majority of Movies and TV Shows were released in recent years, reflecting Netflix’s aggressive expansion strategy.

3. Genre Performance

The most common genres across the platform are:

International Movies

Dramas

Comedies

Documentaries

This highlights strong demand for globally diverse and narrative-driven content.

4. Audience Targeting (Ratings)

Most titles fall under TV-MA, TV-14, and TV-PG ratings.

Movies additionally show high volumes of PG-13 and R-rated content.

Netflix primarily targets teen and adult audiences.

5. Regional Content Distribution

United States leads in content production, followed by India and the United Kingdom.

These three countries account for a significant share of Netflix’s total catalog.

6. Regional Genre Preferences

United States: Strong presence in Documentaries and Comedies

India: High concentration of Dramas and Comedies

United Kingdom: Greater focus on Documentary content

This indicates that regional preferences vary significantly and require localized content strategies.

7. Creator Concentration

A limited number of directors contribute a disproportionately high number of titles.

This suggests Netflix’s tendency to collaborate repeatedly with proven creators, particularly in niche genres.
